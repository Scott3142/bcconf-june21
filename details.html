Keynote Speakers

Becoming Extraordinary

Colin Jackson

Olympic Athlete and Sports Commentator

With an outstanding competitive career behind him Colin is the perfect choice to start the day by sharing his motivational story.

Colin will touch on how he took his eye off the ball and didn't win gold when it mattered, he combines an inspirational story with life-lessons on how to cope with anxiety and mental health.


Andrew McMillan

Customer Service Specialist

Andrew McMillan’s role in defining and growing the John Lewis brand has been key to its success today.

Join the session to listen to Andrew discuss his three decades at the organisation and his insights on creating a distinct customer experience, leading teams, and engaging employees.


Breakout Speakers

People Centered, Team Player, Inclusive, Inspirational, Innovative, Passionate

Chris Probert

Innovation Specialist, Welsh Government

Join Innovation Specialist, Chris Probert as he provides a fascinating insight into Industry 4.0 and the latest exciting and thought provoking emerging technologies.

Chris will focus on the process of innovating and offer up some inspiring examples of risk taking and disruptive innovations that are already having an impact on the way we view the future.


Susie Ramroop

Coach and author of "Be The Leader You Want To See"

If you’re breathing and a high performer, you have probably suffered from impostor syndrome, experiencing fear that you are going to be found out at any moment.

Having suffered from this for most of my corporate career, I get it, but I have an alternative; not a temporary potion that will wear off in a few days. Want to hear it?


Olly Newton

Executive Director, The Edge Foundation

Olly Newton spent 12 years in the Department for Education working on policies including 14-19 diplomas, raising the participation age and finally as Head of Apprenticeship Strategy. Olly is now Executive Director of the Edge Foundation, where he oversees a programme of primary research, is lead author on all of the charity’s policy reports and runs the Edge Future Learning delivery programme for schools and colleges. In his spare time, Olly volunteers with the Scout Association, is Enterprise Adviser for Firth Park Academy and supports community theatre in Sheffield.

Olly will share insights into the work of The Edge Foundation within the devolved nations and explore how as practitioners we can make education relevant in a turbulent and ever-changing world.


Dean Stokes

Education Adoption Manager (EMEA), Google

Dean Stokes is the Education Adoption Manager at Google and co-hosts the What Do They Do podcast.

Learn about how Google tackles big challenges by working on solutions which don't exist yet and how their culture of innovation could work in your classroom. Think about the challenges you and your students are facing to apply some of the concepts you'll hear about in this session. This will be a short presentation followed by time for Q&A.


Professor Uzo Iwobi OBE

Specialist Policy Advisor on Equalities to Welsh Government

Originally from Nigeria, Uzo holds a law degree from the University of Nigeria and she qualified as a solicitor and a barrister and was called to the Nigerian Bar. Uzo moved to Wales where she founded Race Council Cymru and served as the chief executive officer and has worked as a Lecturer in Law at the Swansea Law School. Uzo now fulfills a number of roles, including being the specialist policy advisor on equalities to Welsh Government and being part of the Academi Wales board.

Uzo will share some of her experiences of being a female person of colour living in Wales and will explore how we can all be more people-centred and inclusive.


Jayne Jones

Director, Be Business Fit

Jayne is co-director and Founder of Be Business Fit, and is an award winning, people professional. Previously has worked across a variety of sectors and has held senior roles at The Royal Mint, The Office for National Statistics and Hafod Housing and Care Association. Jayne supports a wide range of businesses and individuals throughout the UK to achieve business success through its people. Jayne is a prominent figure in Welsh Volleyball and currently holds the position of Chair of Cardiff Volleyball Club and is Director of Volleyball Wales.

This session will be interactive, encouraging discussion, exploring views and sharing ideas and examples of how you can contribute to Bridgend College’s Mission Statement of “Be All That You Can Be”.


Jeff Greenidge

Director of Diversity (ETF and AoC)

Jeff is currently working with the Association of Colleges (AoC) and the Education Training Foundation (ETF) to support their work and development on diversity.

Living in Wales, Jeff is the newest member of the college’s board of governors and will be sharing insight and thoughts on how we can create a sense of belonging through true inclusion and what this really means.
